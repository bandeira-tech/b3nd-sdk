# B3nd Funding Strategy — The Gist

**TL;DR:** Five experts disagree on how to fund B3nd, but they all agree on the first three moves.

---

## The Situation

B3nd is a working protocol (v0.7.2) with a solo maintainer, no revenue, no community, and extraordinary potential. The Firecat economic model is designed but not built. The "What's in a Message" book is half-written and genuinely compelling. Infrastructure costs are €50/month. The question is: how do you go from here to a team of 4-6 people working on this full-time?

## Five Experts, Five Strategies

| Expert | Strategy | First Move | Main Risk |
|--------|----------|------------|-----------|
| **VC Strategist** | Build traction, then raise $1.5-2.5M seed | Ship an app, get 1K users, apply to YC | VC equity conflicts with open/nonprofit vision |
| **Open-Source Advisor** | Stack grants + sponsors + consulting | Apply to NLnet/Sovereign Tech Fund today | Grants are slow; consulting pulls founder from building |
| **Token Economist** | SAFT token sale for $2-5M via Firecat | Launch testnet, build community of node operators | Regulatory risk; attracts speculators |
| **Design Lead** | Hire a designer first, build a demo app | €3-5K contract for one beautiful application | A demo without engineering depth is vaporware |
| **Community Builder** | Spend 6 months on DevRel before hiring | Blog series, conference talks, Discord, bounties | 6 months of no engineering progress |

## Where All Five Agree

1. **Build a demo app.** Every funding path requires something a human can see working in 2 minutes. Not the SDK — an *application*.

2. **Publish the book.** "What's in a Message" is the best asset you have for every audience. Serialize it publicly. Now.

3. **Apply for EU grants immediately.** NLnet NGI Zero and Sovereign Tech Fund are almost purpose-built for B3nd's pitch. €50-200K, rolling deadlines, minimal strings.

4. **Don't lead with tokens.** Build protocol adoption first. Firecat is Phase 2.

5. **Fix the bus factor.** First hire = full-stack engineer who can independently own a major subsystem.

## The Numbers

- **Minimum viable team:** Founder + 1 engineer + 1 contract designer = €8-13K/month
- **Low-hanging grants:** NLnet (€5-50K), Sovereign Tech Fund (€50-300K), Mozilla Builders ($25-75K)
- **Current burn rate:** ~€50/month infrastructure
- **First milestone:** Demo app + 2-minute video + grant applications submitted

## The One Thing to Do Today

Apply to NLnet NGI Zero. It costs nothing, takes a few days of writing, has rolling deadlines, and is exactly designed for open-source data sovereignty protocols. Even if everything else in this document is wrong, that grant application should exist.
